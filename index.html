FAQ

Questions people ask about MyHealthPilot.

These answers explain what the app is for, how it can help, and where the boundaries are.

What is MyHealthPilot?

MyHealthPilot is an AI health research agent built with GPs. It helps people turn confusing symptoms into structured notes, research-backed summaries, monitoring plans, and reports for GP or clinician discussions.

Is MyHealthPilot a symptom checker?

MyHealthPilot is not positioned as a diagnostic symptom checker. It supports symptom journaling, symptom tracking, saved health reports, research translation, and appointment preparation.

Does MyHealthPilot diagnose symptoms?

No. MyHealthPilot does not diagnose conditions, provide treatment decisions, triage emergencies, or replace professional medical care.

Can MyHealthPilot help before a GP appointment?

Yes. MyHealthPilot was built with GP input to help people organise health concerns, research relevant context, track patterns, and prepare clearer reports before speaking to a GP or other qualified professional.

What can I track in MyHealthPilot?

You can track self-entered notes such as severity, duration, frequency, triggers, relieving factors, sleep, activity, medication use, impact on daily activities, related symptoms, and changes over time.

Does MyHealthPilot provide medical advice?

MyHealthPilot provides AI-assisted organisation, journaling, monitoring, and research translation support. Diagnosis, treatment, and medical decisions should be discussed with a qualified professional.

How does MyHealthPilot use research sources?

Where research-backed information is used, MyHealthPilot may use direct research papers where relevant, translate the context into plain English, and show visible source links.

Is MyHealthPilot free?

MyHealthPilot is currently available to download. Future features or access options may change.

Who is MyHealthPilot for?

MyHealthPilot is for people who want a calmer AI-supported way to organise symptoms, track patterns, save health notes, understand research context, and prepare for conversations with health professionals.

What should I do in an emergency?

Do not rely on MyHealthPilot in an emergency. Contact local emergency services or seek urgent professional medical help for urgent, severe, or rapidly worsening symptoms.
